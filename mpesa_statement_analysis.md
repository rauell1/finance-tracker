## 2. Dynamic Behavior of M-PESA & Fuliza Overdraft

AI models must understand the following mechanics governing Safaricom's M-PESA ledger:

### A. Cash Balance vs. Overdraft Representation
- Safaricom does **not** record negative values in the wallet balance. If a transaction requires more cash than the user has, and the user has a Fuliza overdraft limit, the wallet balance is drawn down to exactly `0.00`.
- The shortfall is covered by a companion transaction: `OverDraft of Credit Party` (often sharing the same receipt number or time), which represents the Fuliza loan drawdown.
- In a double-entry database, the **actual cash balance** of the M-PESA account is represented as `cash_balance - outstanding_fuliza_debt`. Consequently, the account balance is allowed to go negative (down to `-1,500.00` KES or the specific Fuliza limit).

### B. Auto-Deduction and Repayment
- When any funds are paid into M-PESA (deposits, transfers, sendwave, remittances) and there is an active Fuliza overdraft, Safaricom immediately triggers an auto-deduction.
- This auto-deduction appears as `OD Loan Repayment to 232323 M-PESA Overdraw` (the Withdrawn column shows the repayment, and the Balance column updates accordingly).
- Repayments can also be funded directly from a bank account via Paybill `232323`. In this case, the M-PESA ledger shows the repayment (`OD Loan Repayment`), but there is **no** corresponding deposit/income on M-PESA because the funds went directly from the bank to the loan account.

## 3. Core Database Modeling Rule
To prevent double-counting and ledger drift:
1. **Expenses**: Record the primary expense (e.g. paying merchant, paybill, send money) and its associated transaction fees.
2. **Incomes**: Record all received money and deposits.
3. **Fuliza Drawdown**: Do **NOT** record a separate income transaction for the overdraft drawdown. The negative balance of the cash account naturally represents the borrowed funds.
4. **Fuliza Repayment**: Do **NOT** record explicit `Fuliza repayment` or `OD Loan Repayment` SMS as cash expenses in the database. Doing so double-counts the spending since the original expense was already recorded. Repayments should only update the outstanding debt balance in the `debts` table.
